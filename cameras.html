♥ I flipping love this camera ♥

It's like so small. The flash is so bright and most of the time you can't even use it without the flash. And the pictures have a really nice look.

♥ RB67 and RZ67 ♥

These Pictures were made with my RZ-67 and some 35mm Film in the big Film loaders. The only downside to this is that you "waste" a lot of film. But the image covers the whole negative which looks quite nice.

♥ Leica MD-2 ♥

Prob my most valuable camera (that I got for free -- Thank you School yipiee). It's fully mechanical it has no viewfinder and you have to measure (or guess) everything. Even the lens you have to guess how far something is to achieve sharpness. It's really hard and I still struggle with that a lot but just using the camera is really satisfying and the overall feel is just amazing. Also the quality of glass that is available is just amazing (but so so expensive sadly).

♥ Polaroid SX-70 ♥

Very cool camera but I struggle a lot to take good quality pictures with it. It's kind of a gamble if they come out well or not. Also the film is really expensive (an 8 pack is like 24$ jeez). Also most of the polaroids aren't scanned in. So I only have currently a small selection of images. (3 Images)
What is pretty cool tho is that there are calculators out there so you can exactly see when the camera was made (it's not a real calculator it just takes the Serial Number and it tells you when it's made lol).

Mine was made on the 23rd of March in 1979 and has the serial number 5C947066867.
If you also have a Polaroid Camera you can check the serial number here. You can also see on which shift was made and the exact model of the camera which I find pretty cool.




♥ Nikon F100 ♥

Very reliable and my most used camera. It has very modern features like autofocus, a lightmeter and some other nice features. Production ended in 2006 so I believe it's my "newest" analog camera. The lenses are also very cheap and very good. And I bought an adapter so I can use the old Nikon lenses on my digital camera.
